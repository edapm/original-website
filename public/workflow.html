My Favourite Apps

  Notion

Notion is a kind-of all-in-one notetaking app. I first discovered it via Ali Abdaal , and it is a great app. You can organise all your pages via "databases", and it has a a drag-and-drop-like interface to reorganise elements. However, I find it can lack flexibility, which is why I started using Obsidian. It's free, with unlimited pages, although you're limited to a 5MB upload size for attachments.

  eM Client

eM Client is the email client I use. I prefer it to Thunderbird and Outlook, as it has an uncluttered layout, allowing me to easily read and organise my emails. You can change the theme (light or dark), and have up to two accounts on the free license. However, if you want more than this you have to buy the pro license, but it's not a subscription so you only have to buy it once. They've just released eM Client 8; check it out at emclient.com .

  Obsidian

I discovered Obsidian on Keep Productive , and it was started by the creators of Dynalist as a lockdown project. It's similar to Roam Research, but everything is stored locally, as Markdown (.md) files. It's free for personal use, although it's still in beta. Like Roam, it has a graph view to show all your notes' connections with each other. It's also highly customisable, and you can change colours and the design by adding a custom CSS file. Check it out at obsidian.md .

  Atom

Atom is the text editor I use (I coded this website on it!). It's based on Electron , which was built specifically for the project. It was originally created by GitHub , and is now open-source. Although many people prefer Visual Studio Code , also made on Electron, I prefer Atom as it has a more minimalistic layout. It's Git and GitHub integrations are great too, and there are lots of community themes and packages. Find out more at atom.io .

  Firefox

Firefox is the browser I use. I like it because it isn't a memory hogger (Chrome!), and it's open source. Although it's extensions aren't as good as some of Chrome's, it has a fairly good offering, and is very customisable. I also use Brave sometimes.
